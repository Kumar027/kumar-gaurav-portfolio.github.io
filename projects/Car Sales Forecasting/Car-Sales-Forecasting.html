Project Summary

Full Project Description

1. Project Background

Using historical distribution data, this analysis uncovers patterns across years, quarters and models ‚Äî highlighting rise-peak-decline cycles, market share shifts and supply/demand mismatches.

2. Key Objectives
  • Analyze historical wholesale and retail sales (model & quarter level)
  • Detect oversupplied and undersupplied models; map inventory stress
  • Produce annual and quarterly forecasts (2026‚Äì2028)
  • Provide recommendations for production planning and sales alignment
3. Insights from the Analysis
A. Sales Trends Over the Years

Sales grew from ~430k units to a peak near 1,000k around year 10, then declined to ~230k by year 17 ‚Äî signalling structural market shifts needing strategic response.

B. Model-Level Performance

Strong performers include Discovery, Range Rover (and Sport/Evoque) while models like Defender and some Jaguar SUVs show excess wholesale but weak retail movement.

C. Seasonal Analysis

Retail shows Q1 strength, Q2 dip, Q3 recovery and Q4 peak. Wholesale ramps and often peaks in Q4, making Q4 a critical window for inventory and marketing alignment.

D. Supply Gap Analysis
  • Top oversupplied: Discovery Sport (+5,072 units), F-Pace, E-Pace series
  • Top undersupplied: Range Rover Sport (‚àí19,043 units), Discovery, Range Rover, Defender

5. Strategic Recommendations
  1. Prioritise production for high-demand models (Range Rover family, Discovery).
  2. Reduce wholesale shipments for slow-moving models to avoid dealer overstock.
  3. Use combined annual + quarterly forecasts to synchronise manufacturing and marketing.
  4. Focus Q4 campaigns and inventory placement to capture peak demand.
  5. Revisit product mix and pricing strategy to address the long-term sales decline.
