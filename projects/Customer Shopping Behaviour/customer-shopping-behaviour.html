Project Snapshot

This project analyses the shopping behaviour of 3,900 customers across multiple product categories. The goal: understand spending patterns, customer segments, subscription impact, product category performance, seasonal trends, and drivers of retention. The insights feed marketing, product and retention strategies.

Dataset Summary

  • Rows: 3,900
  • Columns: 18
  • Key fields: Age, Gender, Location, Subscription Status, Category, Item, Amount, Season, Size, Rating, Shipping Type
  • Missing values handled: 37 missing review ratings

Tools & Tech Stack

Python Pandas NumPy Matplotlib / Seaborn MySQL Power BI Excel

Workflow (high-level)

  1. Load & inspect raw data in Python; perform EDA
  2. Clean data (missing values, types, outliers) and feature engineer (age group, purchase frequency)
  3. Load cleaned tables to MySQL and run business queries for KPIs
  4. Connect MySQL to Power BI and design interactive dashboard
  5. Prepare stakeholder PPT and deliver recommendations

Business Problem & Goal

Balanced focus: understand customer behaviour, raise subscription adoption, and identify category-level opportunities to increase revenue and retention.

Key Questions Answered (SQL & BI)

  • Revenue and average order value by gender and age group
  • Subscription vs. non-subscription spend
  • Top performing categories and discount-sensitive products
  • Loyal vs returning vs new customer patterns
  • Shipping preference impacts and seasonal trends

Dashboard & Visuals

Interactive dashboard includes KPIs: total revenue, AOV, subscription conversion, repeat buyer rate, category-wise sales, seasonal filters and customer-segmentation slicers.

Key Insights

  • Only 27% of customers are subscribers ‚Äî a 73% conversion opportunity.
  • Female customers spend ~12% more per transaction on average (AOV ‚âà $60.25).
  • Clothing and Accessories account for the largest revenue share; Footwear and Outerwear underperform.
  • Medium & Large sizes represent ~72% of size demand.
  • Spring is the strongest season for sales; Summer is weakest.
  • Young Adults are the most profitable segment; free shipping is the most preferred option.

Recommendations

  • Run targeted subscription campaigns for Young Adults and Middle-aged females using bundles and trial offers.
  • Promote Footwear and Outerwear with seasonal bundles and discounts to boost adoption.
  • Introduce loyalty tiers for repeat customers to increase CLV.
  • Optimize inventory for M & L sizes; align procurement with Spring peak demand.

  • Projected 12‚Äì18% improvement in subscription conversion after targeted campaigns.
  • Estimated 8‚Äì10% uplift in category sales with optimized promotions.
  • Improved CLV and retention from loyalty programs and better targeting.
